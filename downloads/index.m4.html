The Continental Drift Observation Project is a programme whereby students of secondary school age (11-18) go out once a year and use an accurate GNSS receiver to measure the position of a chosen fixed point. In Europe, they see the effect of the drift after two years. Over their remaining school career they see that the Eurasian continent is moving north-east at about three centimetres per year.
